    Technique Drills

    Focus on refining your swimming strokes (freestyle, backstroke) through specific drills, such as practicing proper arm movements, body position, and breathing techniques.

    Endurance Sets

    Incorporate continuous swimming sessions, starting with 10-15 minutes at a comfortable pace, gradually increasing the duration as your stamina improves.

    Strength Training (Dry Land)

    Include core and upper body exercises, such as planks and resistance band rows, to build strength that supports your swimming performance.

    Technique Drills Day (Freestyle Drill, Kickboard Kicking, Backstroke Practice)

    01 - Freestyle Drill: Practice the basic freestyle stroke, focusing on your arm movement, body position, and breathing technique.


    02 - Kickboard Kicking: Use a kickboard to improve your kick technique. Alternate between flutter kicks and dolphin kicks.


    03 - Backstroke Practice: Swim backstroke to enhance your body position and coordination.


    Endurance Sets Day (Intervals, Continuous Swimming)

    01 - Continuous Swimming: Start with 10-15 minutes of easy swimming at a steady pace. Gradually increase the duration as you become more comfortable.


    02 - Intervals: Swim 4-6 lengths of the pool (freestyle) with short breaks in between (e.g., 30 seconds to 1 minute rest).


    Strength Training (Dry Land) Day (Leg Exercises, Upper Body Strength, Core Exercises)

    01 - Core Exercises: Planks, sit-ups, and Russian twists to build core strength, which is essential for swimming stability.


    02 - Upper Body Strength: Resistance band exercises, such as rows and shoulder presses, to strengthen your arms and shoulders.


    03 - Leg Exercises: Squats and lunges to develop leg strength for powerful kicks.
